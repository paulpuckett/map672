Re: Pickles, humanity has attempted, over time, to understand and explain the world in which it finds itself. It has used myth, philosophy, logic, and eventually science and mathematics in order to understand why: both the philosophical why as well as the empirical why. Maps are part of this revolution as they were first simply used in an attempt to understand and describe the world, consigning certain mounts or places to gods and creating means of safe(r) navigation. Now, with the digital revolution we've attempted to usurp creation itself via the digital evolution/revolution, recreating the world via the computer and programming. From the Turing Test to the Uncanny Valley, to cloud computing evolving to look as much like synapses as nodes. Life imitates Art far more than Art imitates Life, which is just a dramatic way of saying I can order pizza via emoji on my cell and with digital maps, the delivery person can find me. Thanks Newton et al. #Principia2Pepperoni
